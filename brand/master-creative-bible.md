# MIA & TEO LEK & LÆR – MASTER CREATIVE BIBLE

## PROJECT OVERVIEW

Mia & Teo Lek & Lær is the children's universe inside the Little Montessori Explorers (LME) platform.

The goal is to create a premium Norwegian/English learning and entertainment experience inspired by:

* NRK Super
* Disney Pixar
* Duolingo Kids
* Educational adventure games

The platform must feel alive, magical, safe, inspiring and exciting to explore.

NOT a school website.

NOT a traditional Montessori website.

NOT a generic children's learning platform.

It should feel like entering a living world where children learn through stories, exploration, games, videos and adventures.

# CHARACTER LOCK

Mia and Teo have locked character identity. Their approved faces must not be redesigned when making new course images, banners, posters, animations, or videos. New assets may change pose, environment, camera angle, activity, and age-appropriate clothing details, but they must preserve the same recognizable fictional cartoon children from the approved character reference or locked character prompt. Rejected course generations are not character references and must not be used to define their faces.

# MONTESSORI MATERIAL ACCURACY

Montessori materials must never be invented.

When a scene shows Montessori or Practical Life work, use only real, nameable materials and activities.

Allowed Practical Life examples:

* Pouring water or grains with small pitchers
* Spooning or transferring beans between bowls
* Washing a table with sponge and cloth
* Polishing
* Sweeping
* Food preparation
* Hand washing
* Napkin folding
* Dressing frames for buttons, buckles, snaps, bows, and laces

Never use:

* Generic wooden blocks as Montessori Practical Life material
* Fantasy tools
* Random sorting boards
* Invented trays
* Decorative symbols
* Pseudo-Montessori props
* Materials that cannot be identified as a real Practical Life exercise

Mia and Teo should stay in the established illustrated premium 3D Mia & Teo style with locked character identity. The materials must be realistic and pedagogically accurate; the children should not become photorealistic and their faces should not be redesigned.
