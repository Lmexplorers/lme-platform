# Mia & Teo Age Progression

Use this skill to create a consistent set of **Mia and Teo** character images and related educational visuals across school-age groups for diaries, planners, worksheets, sticker packs, learning materials, banners, learning-world assets, and the “Mia & Teo Lek & Lær” universe.

This workflow is optimized for visual consistency, age progression, brand-world consistency, and safe handling of reference images whose faces may be blurred or otherwise unsuitable for direct reproduction. For full brand, category, environment, homepage, and banner rules, read `references/master-creative-bible.md` before producing new Mia & Teo universe assets beyond a simple character cutout.

## When to Use

Use this skill when the user asks to generate Mia and Teo in different ages, school grades, planner scenes, sticker sets, recurring educational assets, banners, homepage/world scenes, games/video/audio-book/mission category visuals, or learning-tree environments. Also use it when the user provides reference images of Mia and Teo and wants new images that keep the same character universe while changing age, pose, clothing, or scene.

## Core Rule

Preserve **locked character identity** for Mia and Teo. When the user has approved a character reference or locked character prompt, Mia and Teo must keep the same face structure, expression family, hair silhouette, proportions, and identity cues across course images, banners, posters, and videos. Do not redesign their faces for new scenes.

If uploaded references contain blurred, blocked, censored, low-quality, or privacy-protected faces, do not reconstruct or infer the face from the blur. Use the approved locked character prompt and approved unblurred character sources when available. If only rejected course images are available, treat them only as scene/material drafts and never as face references.

> Generate Mia and Teo as the same approved fictional cartoon characters from the locked character identity. Do not create new faces, do not copy blur/censor artifacts, and do not use rejected generated images as character identity references.

## Brand Universe Rules

Mia & Teo Lek & Lær is the children’s universe inside Little Montessori Explorers. It should feel like a premium Norwegian/English learning-adventure world inspired by safe children’s entertainment, exploration, stories, games, videos, and adventures rather than like a generic school website.

| Area | Rule |
|---|---|
| Core feeling | Alive, magical, safe, inspiring, exciting to explore, premium, and educational. |
| Primary colours | Cerise pink, lime green, bright sky blue, and lemon yellow. |
| Secondary colours | Soft cream, warm wood tones, and nature greens. |
| Main world symbol | A giant magical Learning Tree with warm lanterns, treehouse details, rope bridges, learning spaces, and a cozy atmosphere. |
| Environment style | Disney/Pixar-inspired premium animation quality with NRK Super warmth, nature, adventure, discovery, warm light, depth, and storytelling. |
| Avoid | Generic school-site feeling, traditional Montessori-site feeling, generic learning-platform visuals, clutter, overcrowded backgrounds, random castles, generic fairy-tale kingdoms, white boxes, white text panels, and generic stock illustrations. |

For homepage and content-area signs, use large wooden signs with all-caps Norwegian labels: **EVENTYR**, **SPILL**, **VIDEOER**, **LYDBØKER**, and **OPPDRAG**. Read `references/master-creative-bible.md` whenever the task involves the broader world, banners, category pages, homepage scenes, or exact content-area wording.

## Typography Rule

All text for the “Mia & Teo Lek & Lær” universe must use **SassoonMontessori** from `templates/SASSM___.TTF` as the body/standard font. Use **Playpen Sans** for headings and playful heading-style titles, especially where the rounded single-storey lowercase “a” is important. Never use generic children’s fonts, corporate fonts, generic Canva fonts, or fonts with a two-storey lowercase “a” when a single-storey child-friendly “a” is required. For posters/plansjer with precise Norwegian titles, generate or compose the final title so the required font remains exact and brand-consistent.

## Montessori Material Accuracy Rule

When an asset includes Montessori materials, especially **Praktisk liv / Practical Life**, do not invent “Montessori-like” objects. Use only real, nameable Montessori or everyday Practical Life materials and activities, such as pouring water or grains with small pitchers, spooning/transferring beans between bowls, washing a table with sponge and cloth, polishing, sweeping, food preparation, hand washing, napkin folding, or dressing frames for buttons, buckles, snaps, bows, and laces. Avoid generic wooden blocks, fantasy learning props, random sorting boards, invented trays, decorative symbols, unreadable pseudo-materials, and objects that cannot be identified as a real Practical Life exercise.

For Practical Life scenes, Mia and Teo remain stylized fictional Mia & Teo characters in the established premium 3D children’s universe; only the materials must be realistic, recognizable, and pedagogically accurate.

| Text Type | Required Font |
|---|---|
| Body text, standard labels, captions, learning copy | SassoonMontessori (`templates/SASSM___.TTF`) |
| Headings and playful heading-style titles | Playpen Sans, or SassoonMontessori if the user wants the softer school-material look |

## Workflow

### 1. Read the User’s Intent

Determine whether the user wants a character pair, separate individual characters, a sticker sheet, scene set, or age progression. If they ask generally to “generate Mia and Teo in the age groups,” create four separate final images by default, one per age group.

Ask only if a blocking requirement is missing, such as exact output format, number of images, transparent versus white background, or whether individual characters must be separated. Otherwise proceed with reasonable defaults.

### 2. Handle References Safely

When reference images are provided, first classify them as either approved character references or scene/material references. Use approved character references to preserve locked Mia and Teo identity. Use rejected course images only for composition, environment, or material placement. If faces are blurred or blocked, explicitly instruct generation to avoid copied, blurred, censored, blank, reconstructed, or newly redesigned faces, and rely on the locked character prompt instead.

For image generation prompts, include language such as:

```text
Use the provided images only as style and character-reference anchors, not as facial identity references. Generate original fictional cartoon faces. Avoid blurred faces, censor blocks, blank rectangles, face coverings, or copied faces.
```

If the model carries over blur blocks from references, regenerate without the reference images and describe the character anchors textually instead. Do not repeatedly edit the same blur artifact unless the first correction succeeds clearly.

### 3. Build the Prompt Set

Create one prompt per age group or asset type. Each prompt should include subject, composition, style, brand-world cues, text constraints, and avoid-list. Use English prompts for image generation even when the user is working in Norwegian. For broader banners or world scenes, include the Learning Tree, warm lanterns, treehouse/rope-bridge details, natural negative space for text, wooden signs when category labels are needed, warm light, depth, and a strong sense of adventure.

A reliable prompt structure is:

```text
Create a polished 3D children's book sticker-style character illustration for a Norwegian school diary and learning universe.
Subject: Mia and Teo as [age group] fictional children/teenagers, standing side by side as best friends, never romantic, equal visual importance.
Mia: [age-appropriate Mia anchors].
Teo: [age-appropriate Teo anchors].
Composition: full body, centered pair, both visible from head to shoes, clean sticker asset silhouette, generous safe margin, simple positive pose.
Style: high-end 3D educational publishing illustration, cute Scandinavian planner-sticker aesthetic, soft natural daylight, bright clean colors, subtle soft shadow, thick white sticker outline.
Text/content to render: no text, no numbers, no labels. If text is later needed in a Mia & Teo educational asset, use SassoonMontessori (`templates/SASSM___.TTF`) or Playpen Sans for headings only when requested.
Constraints: complete characters, no cropped limbs, no extra people, no clutter.
Avoid: blurred faces, censor blocks, blank rectangles, face coverings, masks, romance, adult styling, distorted hands, distorted feet, extra fingers, watermark, written words.
```

### 4. Recommended Default Deliverables

Unless the user requests otherwise, deliver separate PNG files with clear names. Use a square aspect ratio for general planner/sticker assets.

| File Naming Pattern | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `mia_teo_3_5.png` | Mia and Teo for ages 3–5 when preschool visuals are requested. |
| `mia_teo_6_8.png` | Mia and Teo for ages 6–8. |
| `mia_teo_9_11.png` | Mia and Teo for ages 9–11. |
| `mia_teo_12_13.png` | Mia and Teo for ages 12–13. |
| `mia_teo_14_16.png` | Mia and Teo for ages 14–16. |
| `mia_teo_16_18.png` | Mia and Teo for ages 16–18 when mentor/role-model visuals are requested. |

Use transparent background when the user needs stickers, compositing, worksheets, or Canva-style assets. Use a clean white background when faster review or general visual presentation is enough.

### 5. Quality Check Before Delivery

Do a lightweight pass/fail check only. Confirm that each image has the correct age direction, both characters are complete, Mia and Teo remain recognizably the same approved characters from the locked prompt/reference, text follows the required typography when present, no face blur or censor block has carried over, and the relationship reads as best friends/adventure partners rather than romantic.

If a fatal issue appears, fix the single most important issue once. If references cause face blocks to repeat, regenerate from text-only anchors.

## Common Failure Modes and Fixes

| Failure | Likely Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Face is blurred or covered by a rectangle. | The reference image contained a blur/censor area. | Regenerate without image references and describe anchors textually. |
| Characters look romantic. | Pose language is too intimate. | Add “best friends, never romantic, equal visual importance, supportive school friendship.” |
| Age groups look too similar. | Prompt did not change clothing/proportions enough. | Strengthen age-specific clothing and body-proportion cues. |
| Teen versions look too adult. | Fashion or pose became too mature. | Add “wholesome, age-appropriate school outfit, clearly teenage, not adult.” |
| Sticker asset is hard to reuse. | Background too busy or limbs cropped. | Add “full body, generous safe margin, clean white background, thick white sticker outline.” |

## Delivery Message Pattern

When finished, deliver the generated images directly and summarize the age mapping or asset purpose in a compact table. Mention any important assumption, such as white background versus transparent background. If the deliverable contains text, state that SassoonMontessori was used for body/standard text and Playpen Sans for headings when applicable. Do not over-explain the generation process.

Use language like:

```text
Ferdig — jeg har laget separate PNG-bilder av Mia og Teo i de avtalte aldersgruppene. De er laget som helkropps sticker-/planner-assets med konsekvente karakterankere, aldersjustert stil og Mia & Teo Lek & Lær-profilen.
```
